Correcting Errors in Sentences Written by Non-native Speakers of English

Sentences written by non-native speakers of English may contain different types of errors. The goal of this HIT is to correct errors in sentences produced by non-native English speakers in order to establish better quality data and give a feedback to non-native speakers of English.

The process of correcting different types of errors in this data involves annotation of common English as Second Language (ESL) errors. Sentences annotated with common ESL errors may be shared with researches who are working on second language acquisition and automated spelling correction.

We are looking for several types of ESL errors including:

  • Noun Related Errors
    • Article error
    • Other determiners error
    • Noun number error
    • Noun of noun error
    • Pronoun error
  • Proposition Related Errors
  • Verb Related Errors
    • Subject verb agreement error
    • Tense error
    • Verb form error
  • Other Errors
    • Spelling / Capitalization error
    • Word form error
    • Adjective related error
    • Sentence fragment error
    • Awkward phrase in English error

Instructions: This hit is a multistep process. Please follow instructions carefully. General instructions are described below. Additionally, more specific instructions related to the correction of specific error types will be provided on every tab.

  • Each type of error must be corrected on a corresponding tab e.g., spelling and capitalization errors should be corrected by clicking on 'Spelling/Capitalization' tab, pronoun errors should be corrected by clicking on corresponding 'Pronouns' tab.
  • The order in which correction can be done is not restricted e.g., you can first correct adjective related errors and then article errors. You can switch between tabs at any time. All errors that have been corrected on previous tabs are reflected on a current tab.
  • You can make a correction by clicking on an incorrect word (or a phrase) and type the correct version of the word in the editbox that appears. If any word is missing, you should click on the word following the missing word and type two words into the editbox. If any word must be deleted (e.g., unnecessary article before a noun), you need to select a phrase Ð noun with preceding article and retype the noun without any article.

Do not forget to correct errors ONLY on corresponding error tab! Good luck!
